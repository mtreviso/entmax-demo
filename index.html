Mathematical Definition of α-entmax

For \(\alpha > 1\), the \(\alpha\)-entmax transformation of a score vector \(\mathbf{z} \in \mathbb{R}^m\) is defined as:

\[ \alpha\text{-entmax}(\mathbf{z}) = \arg\max_{\mathbf{p} \in \triangle^m} \Bigl(\mathbf{p}^\top \mathbf{z} + H_\alpha(\mathbf{p})\Bigr), \quad \triangle^m = \bigl\{\mathbf{p} \in \mathbb{R}^m_{\ge 0}:\sum_i p_i = 1\bigr\}, \]

where \(H_\alpha(\mathbf{p})\) is the Tsallis(\(\alpha\)) entropy. A known closed form for \(\alpha\text{-entmax}\) with \(\alpha > 1\) is:

\[ p_i^\star = \bigl[(\alpha-1)\,z_i - \tau(\mathbf{z})\bigr]_+^{\tfrac{1}{\alpha-1}}, \quad \sum_{i=1}^m p_i^\star = 1, \quad [\cdot]_+ = \max(0,\cdot). \]

Here, \(\tau(\mathbf{z})\) is chosen so that \(\mathbf{p}^\star\) sums to 1.
